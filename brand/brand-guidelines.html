01 · The Brand Freyja Labs

The brand.

Freyja Labs is a national consulting firm delivering customized, hands-on professional development.

Who we are

Freyja Labs designs and delivers professional development for school districts, schools, non-traditional education spaces, and corporate partners. Every engagement is tailored to the audience and built on a "small tell, lots of do" philosophy — participants build, test, and iterate rather than passively receive information.

The name

Freyja is the Norse goddess of wisdom, foresight, and transformation — a figure who bridges worlds and enables others to see further. "Labs" signals experimentation, active work, and a maker-oriented approach. Together, the name communicates wisdom applied through hands-on practice.

The mark

Our mark is a meristem — the growth zone at the tip of every plant where new cells originate. Five primordia spiral around a central node, each at a different stage of development. The asymmetry is intentional: growth is uneven, directional, and never complete.

What we promise

Tailored to the audience. Hands-on by design. Research-grounded. Ahead of the curve. These are not features but commitments — every deliverable that carries the Freyja Labs name should be measurable against them.

02 · The Mark Freyja Labs

The mark.

A meristem in cross-section. Five primordia, one growing point, and the breath of asymmetry that makes it alive.

Construction

Eight elements: a forest disc anchoring the composition, five elliptical primordia spiraling around it, and a layered focal point at the center. The smallest, most opaque leaf sits at twelve o'clock; the largest, most translucent at nine. The upper-left quadrant is intentionally empty — that gap is what gives the mark its directional energy.

Why this mark

A meristem is where every new cell of a plant originates. It is the literal location of growth. The mark says — without words — what the brand promises: tailored, ongoing, organic development. The asymmetry signals that growth is never finished and never identical from one engagement to the next.

03 · Logo System Freyja Labs

Logo system.

Three forms, two color treatments, calibrated for every context.

Mark only

Color · light backgroundsReversed · dark backgrounds

Horizontal lockup

Color · light backgroundsReversed · dark backgrounds

When to use which

Lockup for any first-impression context: website headers, business cards, letterhead, presentation cover slides, social media banners. Lead with the full lockup whenever the brand is being introduced.

Mark only for repeat-impression and small contexts: favicons, app icons, social profile pictures, email signature, footer return marks, embroidery, watermarks, or any time the lockup would feel redundant or won't fit at legible size.

04 · Clear Space & Misuse Freyja Labs

Clear space & misuse.

The mark needs room to breathe. These rules protect that.

Minimum clear space

Maintain clear space around the mark equal to the radius of its central forest disc. No competing elements — text, photography, other logos, or page edges — should encroach on this zone.

Minimum size

The mark renders cleanly at 16×16px (favicon size) using the simplified favicon variant. The full mark should not appear smaller than 24×24px on screen or 0.4 inches in print. The horizontal lockup should not appear smaller than 120px wide on screen or 1.25 inches in print.

Misuse

Don't recolor the mark

Forest, sage, cream, and dark accent are non-negotiable. Don't tint the mark to match a host page or sponsor scheme. If the host palette can't accept brand colors, use the white reversed mark on a dark forest background.

Don't rotate, skew, or stretch

The asymmetric spiral has a specific orientation. Rotating it (even by 15° for visual interest) breaks the composition. Preserve aspect ratio at all sizes.

Don't add effects

No drop shadows, glows, gradients, outlines, or embossing. The mark is flat by design.

Don't reconstruct or modify

Don't redraw leaves "cleaner" or symmetrically. Don't replace the central iris with a different motif. Don't add or remove primordia.

Do use the white version on dark

For dark backgrounds, photographs, or video, use the white reversed mark. Maintain enough contrast that the mark reads at a glance.

Do place on solid backgrounds

Keep the area immediately around the mark a single, solid color. Busy backgrounds, photography behind the mark, or patterned fills compromise legibility.

Typography.

A serif and a sans, paired to communicate established authority and modern clarity.

Display & Editorial
Cormorant Garamond  ·  Garalde serif  ·  Christian Thalmann  ·  SIL Open Font License
Aa Bb Cc
Regular — running editorial body
Medium — subheads & pull quotes
Semibold — primary display headings
UI & Body
Outfit  ·  Geometric sans  ·  Indian Type Foundry  ·  SIL Open Font License
Aa Bb Cc
Regular — UI body, descriptions, captions
Medium — UI labels, button text
Semibold — navigation, emphasis, eyebrows

Pairing rules

Cormorant Garamond for headings, brand voice, pull quotes, and any moment that should feel considered or editorial. Use weight 500 or 600. Italic weight 400 is reserved for the tagline and pull quotes.

Outfit for everything else: body text, navigation, buttons, captions, eyebrows, metadata. Use weight 400 for running text, 500 for emphasis, 600 for navigation labels and uppercase eyebrows.

Sentence case on all headings. Reserve all-caps for short labels (eyebrows, navigation, button text) with letter-spacing of 1.5–2px.

Fallbacks

When Cormorant Garamond isn't available: Georgia, Hoefler Text, "Times New Roman", serif. When Outfit isn't available: -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", system-ui, sans-serif.

07 · Voice & Tone Freyja Labs

Voice & tone.

Direct, evidence-first, warm but not soft. Confident without arrogance.

Principles

Lead with what we do, not what we are. Verbs over nouns. "We design hands-on workshops" over "We are a hands-on workshop firm."

Specific beats generic. "Three districts saw a 28% increase in CS course offerings" not "Many districts have benefited."

Earn every word. Cut filler, intensifiers, and superlatives. If a sentence still works without "very," "really," "truly," or "absolutely" — cut them.

Avoid jargon for jargon's sake. "Capacity building" is fine when it means something specific. "Synergize learning outcomes" is not.

Voice examples

Avoid
We are a passionate, innovative team committed to revolutionizing professional development through cutting-edge methodologies.
Better
We design professional development for school districts. Every engagement is built for the people in the room.
Avoid
Our mission is to empower educators to unlock the potential of their students through transformative learning experiences.
Better
We help teachers do harder things better. The kind of work students remember, not the kind they forget.
Avoid
Leveraging best-in-class research and evidence-based practices, we deliver high-impact solutions tailored to your unique context.
Better
Every design decision points to a citation. Every workshop is built for your district, not adapted from another one.

Words we use

Build. Design. Iterate. Test. Anchor. Tailor. Ground. Grow. Specific. Direct. Hands-on. Evidence. Practice. Context. Cohort. Cycle.

Words we don't

Empower. Unlock. Revolutionary. Cutting-edge. Best-in-class. Synergy. Transform (alone, without specifics). Game-changing. World-class. Holistic. Stakeholder (when "team" or "people" works).
